Critical (Immediate Action)
IKT January — 52 of 193 denied (26.9%)
Highest denial rate of any scheme. 11× higher than CMCHIS core rate. Possible causes: Aadhaar mismatch, beneficiary ineligibility, incorrect procedure code, incomplete documentation.
IKT February — 40 of 168 denied (23.8%)
Denial rate only marginally improved vs January. The problem is structural, not a one-off. Requires a formal process audit with the CMCHIS desk officer and IKT scheme coordinator.
ENT Surgery — 100% denial in January
Only 1 ENT surgery claim submitted and it was denied. Recovered in Feb (4/4 approved) but the January denial should be investigated and the patient re-evaluated for resubmission or appeal.
Warning (Monitor Closely)
OG (Obstetrics) — Denial spike from 0% to 20% in February
3 of 15 OG claims denied in February after zero denials in January. Sudden spikes often indicate a change in documentation requirements, a package code update, or a new admission officer unfamiliar with CMCHIS guidelines.
Hearing Aid — Denial rate increasing month-on-month
1 denial in January (10%), 2 denials in February (10.5%) with higher volume (19 cases). While rate is stable, the absolute number of denied patients is rising with volume. Eligibility criteria should be re-confirmed with staff.
OAE — 9 denials in January despite low volume
16 of 25 approved (64%) in January — a 36% denial rate. Improved significantly in Feb (42/45 = 93.3%). January denials need retrospective review; if procedurally eligible, these may be appellable.

Proven Best Practices from High-Performing TN District Hospitals
Dedicated CMCHIS Ward Coordinator
Tirunelveli GH · Rajaji Madurai GH
Assign one CMCHIS-trained staff member per 30 beds to handle eligibility checks, card swiping, and claim submission at ward level — not just at a central desk. This removes the bottleneck where patients wait 24–48 hours for the desk officer to visit the ward.
Result: 28% increase in coded admissions within 6 months
Pre-Auth Protocol Before Every Elective Surgery
Nagapattinam DH · Cuddalore DH
Nagapattinam DH mandates CMCHIS pre-authorisation 24 hours before any elective procedure. The surgeon confirms the CMCHIS package code on the OT register. This prevents post-surgery denials from wrong code selection — their top denial cause until 2024.
Result: Pre-auth denials dropped from 18% to 2.1% in 4 months
Monthly Doctor Incentive Dashboard
Salem MKMC GH
Salem MKMC publishes a monthly performance board showing each department's CMCHIS approval rate and denied amounts. Departments with >95% approval receive public recognition at the monthly meeting. This gamification created healthy competition between departments and reduced denials across all units.
Result: Overall denial rate fell from 11% to 3.4% in 8 months
Village Beneficiary Identification Camps
Nagapattinam DH · Tirunelveli GH
Nagapattinam DH runs quarterly camps at primary health centres to identify CMCHIS-eligible patients awaiting elective procedures (hernias, cataracts, orthopaedic fixations). Patients are pre-registered and scheduled to GH in batches, increasing predictable volume by 15–20 cases/month with near-zero denials since eligibility is pre-verified.
Result: +18 additional elective cases/month, 97% approval rate for camp patients
Daily 9 AM Claim Submission Deadline
Rajaji Madurai GH
Rajaji GH enforces a rule: all previous day's discharge claims must be submitted to CMCHIS portal by 9 AM. The CMCHIS desk officer sends a WhatsApp summary to the Medical Superintendent each morning showing yesterday's submission count. This prevents batch submission delays that cause the 48-hour window to lapse.
Result: Average claim-to-submission time reduced from 4.2 days to 18 hours
Cross-District Learning Exchange
Cuddalore DH · Nagapattinam DH
Cuddalore and Nagapattinam DH CMCHIS teams do a quarterly one-day visit exchange — each hospital's CMCHIS officer spends a day observing the other's workflow. This low-cost practice rapidly spreads process innovations between comparable hospitals. MGH should initiate this with Nagapattinam DH, the closest comparable peer.
Result: Cuddalore adopted Nagapattinam's pre-auth workflow, reducing denials by 14%
