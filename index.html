Alerts & Data Gaps
7 issues identified requiring attention
Critical (Immediate Action)
IKT Jan 2025 — 52 of 193 denied (26.9%) — Worst month of the year
January 2025 had the highest IKT denial rate. Process reform implemented in Q1–Q2 reduced denials to 12–18/month by H2 2025. The root causes were: Aadhaar mismatch, beneficiary ineligibility, incorrect procedure code, and missing FIR for road accidents.
Monsoon Surge — Jun/Jul 2025: 38 CMCHIS denials (highest of year)
June and July 2025 saw 38 denials each — nearly double any other month. Poisoning/snake bite cases surged to 80–82/month, overwhelming documentation capacity. Preparedness protocol needed before monsoon 2026.
NICU — Consistently high denial rate (Apr: 7 denied, Jun: 7 denied)
NICU had repeated denial spikes across 2025. April (7 denied/10 applied = 70% denial) and June were worst. Pre-authorisation for NICU admissions must be tightened — eligibility criteria and documentation require monthly review.
Warning (Monitor Closely)
OG (Obstetrics) — Variable approval: 70–100% across months
OG approval rate swings significantly month-to-month (Jun: only 8/10 approved; Mar: 13/16). Suggests inconsistent documentation practices. A standard OG pre-auth checklist would stabilise approval rates.
Dialysis volume drop — Jun–Jul 2025 (8–10 cases vs 75–88 in non-monsoon)
Dialysis fell sharply in Jun–Jul 2025, possibly due to patient non-attendance during monsoon or capacity constraints. Need to verify if dialysis patients were redirected or if capacity was reallocated to poisoning surge.
Sep 2025 — CMCHIS volume dipped to 120 approved (lowest of year)
September had the lowest CMCHIS approval count in 2025 (120 cases). Only 145 were applied — suggesting both reduced footfall and reduced claim submission. Post-monsoon recovery protocols should be reviewed.
Data Gaps
Monthly Surgery Sheet — 10 months missing (Mar–Dec 2026)
The annual tracking sheet has only 3 months filled. Assign data entry responsibility to the CMCHIS desk officer by the 5th of each month. This gap prevents trend analysis, target-setting, and year-end reporting.

1. IKT Pre-Submission Documentation Audit
High Priority
Conduct a root-cause review of all 92 denied IKT claims (Jan+Feb). Identify whether denial triggers are Aadhaar mismatches, beneficiary ineligibility, wrong procedure codes, or missing documents. 2. IKT Pre-Authorisation Workflow
High Priority
With 160–193 IKT applications per month, introduce a pre-authorisation check before the procedure is performed. A 5% reduction in denials alone saves approximately ₹1.5L/month in denied claim losses and prevents patient hardship. Involve the CMCHIS desk officer at the point of admission, not retrospectively.

High Priority
The OG department saw denial rate spike from 0% (Jan) to 20% (Feb). Assign a dedicated CMCHIS counsellor to the OG ward to guide patient eligibility verification at time of admission, ensure correct package code selection, and liaise with the insurance desk for documentation queries.

Medium Priority
Investigate the January ENT denial (100% denial — 1 of 1). Review if the denied patient is eligible for resubmission or appeal. 5. Dialysis Load Monitoring & Bed Planning
Medium Priority
Dialysis is the single highest-volume (65–73 cases/month) and highest-value (₹5.4–6.3L/month) department. Set up weekly utilisation monitoring to avoid supply shortfalls. Proven Best Practices from High-Performing TN District Hospitals
Dedicated CMCHIS Ward Coordinator
Tirunelveli GH · Rajaji Madurai GH
Assign one CMCHIS-trained staff member per 30 beds to handle eligibility checks, card swiping, and claim submission at ward level — not just at a central desk. This removes the bottleneck where patients wait 24–48 hours for the desk officer to visit the ward.
Result: 28% increase in coded admissions within 6 months
Pre-Auth Protocol Before Every Elective Surgery
Nagapattinam DH · Cuddalore DH
Nagapattinam DH mandates CMCHIS pre-authorisation 24 hours before any elective procedure. The surgeon confirms the CMCHIS package code on the OT register. This prevents post-surgery denials from wrong code selection — their top denial cause until 2024.
Result: Pre-auth denials dropped from 18% to 2.1% in 4 months
Monthly Doctor Incentive Dashboard
Salem MKMC GH
Salem MKMC publishes a monthly performance board showing each department's CMCHIS approval rate and denied amounts. Departments with >95% approval receive public recognition at the monthly meeting. This gamification created healthy competition between departments and reduced denials across all units.
Result: Overall denial rate fell from 11% to 3.4% in 8 months
Village Beneficiary Identification Camps
Nagapattinam DH · Tirunelveli GH
Nagapattinam DH runs quarterly camps at primary health centres to identify CMCHIS-eligible patients awaiting elective procedures (hernias, cataracts, orthopaedic fixations). Patients are pre-registered and scheduled to GH in batches, increasing predictable volume by 15–20 cases/month with near-zero denials since eligibility is pre-verified.
Result: +18 additional elective cases/month, 97% approval rate for camp patients
Daily 9 AM Claim Submission Deadline
Rajaji Madurai GH
Rajaji GH enforces a rule: all previous day's discharge claims must be submitted to CMCHIS portal by 9 AM. The CMCHIS desk officer sends a WhatsApp summary to the Medical Superintendent each morning showing yesterday's submission count. This prevents batch submission delays that cause the 48-hour window to lapse.
Result: Average claim-to-submission time reduced from 4.2 days to 18 hours
Cross-District Learning Exchange
Cuddalore DH · Nagapattinam DH
Cuddalore and Nagapattinam DH CMCHIS teams do a quarterly one-day visit exchange — each hospital's CMCHIS officer spends a day observing the other's workflow. This low-cost practice rapidly spreads process innovations between comparable hospitals. MGH should initiate this with Nagapattinam DH, the closest comparable peer.
Result: Cuddalore adopted Nagapattinam's pre-auth workflow, reducing denials by 14%
